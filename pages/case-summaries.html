Case Study 1  ·  Economic Damages

Chemical Cloud Dispersion Analysis

Situation

A plume from a chemical plant fire drifted over residential areas as it dissipated. Local residents were instructed to stay inside until the fire was extinguished. After the fire was extinguished, some residents submitted claims against the chemical company for alleged property damage.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to perform a dispersion analysis utilizing our own internal models and EPA models to determine the path of the plume over time and opine on whether certain residences were in fact outside the modeled path of the plume and their property damage claims were unreasonable.
Case Study 2  ·  Litigation Support

Oil & Gas — FPSO Construction Dispute

Situation

Floating Production Storage and Offloading (FPSO) vessels are essentially floating refineries. A FPSO is docked at an offshore platform and take the crude oil produced and refine it into finished products. The finished products are then offloaded to tanker vessels or barges where the finished products are shipped to their next destinations. These are very complex vessels requiring years to complete. In many cases, construction dispute arises with respect to contested change orders and schedule delays.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to assist in the resolution of contested change orders and schedule delays related to 5 FPSOS. This entailed the review of thousands of documents and records, cost estimating, labor productivity assessments, schedule delay analysis, several currency conversions and claim valuation to the present time. Technical disciplines included structural, fire safety, mechanical, electrical and instrumentation.
Case Study 3  ·  Investigation

Personal Injury — Hot Work and Line Break

Situation

An Oil & Gas midstream company subcontracted plant modifications to accept a new crude oil product. The modifications included making tie-ins to existing piping for the installation of Tee fittings to direct the crude oil to its associated storage tank. A cold machine cut was made to minimize the risk of fire at the tie-ins. This was followed by welding new flange fittings to accept the new Tee fitting. A commercial grade plug was installed to limit vapors to the hot work area. During the welding of the flange fitting, a fire erupted that injured several workers as they jumped off their scaffolding to avoid the fire.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to review the safe work practices of all parties including hot work, line break, platform emergency escape route planning, job safety analysis, safety training records, tools and isolation equipment used, flammable gas detection method and readings, and determine the root cause of the fire.
Case Study 4  ·  Investigation

Oil & Gas Midstream — Oil Storage Tank Fire

Situation

An Oil & Gas midstream company subcontracted the removal of both the floating roof seals to a specialist contractor. Since the storage tank still had product, the owner specified the use of non-sparking tools be used to replace the seal. During the work, vapors ignited and the resultant fire consumed the floating and exterior roofs and its contents.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to review the subcontractor's work processes, hazardous identification training, equipment and tools used. Samples of the storage tank contents were analyzed for flammables not communicated to the subcontractor. Operational status of the existing foam based fire protection system was also assessed. Damage cost assessment was also estimated.
Case Study 5  ·  Litigation Support

Oil & Gas Midstream — Railcar Unloading Contract Dispute

Situation

A newly constructed railcar unloading station was designed to unload several types of crude oil in a specific time frame. During start-up and commissioning, a new heavier crude oil was introduced and the resultant railcar unloading rate was reduced. This issue was not able to be resolved and the parties initiated litigation.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to review the process design of the pump and piping system and opine on whether the design was adequate for all of the types of crude expected to be unloaded at the facility within the original specified unloading rate.
Case Study 6  ·  Economic Damages

Oil & Gas Downstream Refining — Business Interruption

Situation

A refinery experienced a power outage at their coker unit due to an accidental trip of an electrical circuit breaker at the switchyard. The outage lasted approximately two hours. However, the start-up of the coker unit was delayed due to pluggage issues with valves and piping that had to be cleared before the coker could re-start. The company claimed a business interruption loss and equipment damage that could not be resolved. The claim went to litigation.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to investigate the loss and opine on the root cause of the damaged equipment, estimate the cost of repair, determine the amount of lost petroleum coke and estimate the value of the lost coke.
Case Study 7  ·  Economic Damages

Upstream Offshore Platform — Underwater Pipeline Damage

Situation

In the Gulf of Mexico, a tugboat lost power in a storm and dropped anchor in order to avoid striking an offshore production facility. The anchor damaged the sales pipeline from the production facility to shore. The oil and gas producing company claimed damages from not only the damaged pipeline but also the inability to sell natural gas for a period of six months while the pipeline repair was planned and executed.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to determine the cost of repairing the pipeline and to determine the cost of the deferred gas sales. Rimkus utilized "Cash Flow Analysis" techniques to model the gas production capability of the platform to determine the impact of the six month shut down period compared to the production that would have been generated if the incident had not occurred. The damages due to the deferred gas sales were computed and added to the repair cost of the pipeline to resolve the claim.
Case Study 8  ·  Forensic Accounting

Tanker Truck Internal Corrosion

Situation

A stainless steel tanker truck was leased from a leasing company for the purpose of hauling a specific fluid used in the oil and gas industry. The fluid was reportedly compatible with the stainless steel. Following several trips to the rigs, the tanker truck was emptied and cleaned. When the cleaning crew entered the tanker truck, they discovered that the internal surface of the tanker was severely corroded. An insurance claim was submitted to cover the loss.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to review the shipping manifests to determine if and when a chemical entered the tanker truck that caused the corrosion. After review of the manifests for the current and previous lessees, it was determined that an incompatible fluid was hauled by the previous lessee.
Case Study 9  ·  Litigation Support

Wastewater Treatment Plant Energy Optimization Claim

Situation

A newly constructed biological wastewater treatment plant was financed by way of energy savings from discontinuing the operation of the older and less efficient plant. The older plant was at the end of its life and a new plant was designed and constructed to replace it. After start-up and commissioning, it was observed that the contracted energy savings did not materialize. Subsequently, the owner sued the process engineering firm, the design-build contractor and their detail design firm for recovery of the excess energy used and construction costs to remedy.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained by the design-build engineering firm to review the merits of the case and opine on the root cause of the excess energy usage. Our review included the process engineering documents including the plant and equipment sizing and energy savings calculations. Also reviewed were the contract documents as they flowed from the owner to the design build contractor and then to the detail design firm. Our review concluded that the root cause was the incorrect and higher influent loading than estimated by the process engineering firm that resulted in the plant operating essentially continuously and requiring much more energy to operate.
Case Study 10  ·  Investigation

Wastewater Treatment Plant Performance

Situation

A newly constructed biological wastewater treatment plant was alleged unable to meet performance criteria and the owner initiated litigation with all parties involved. One issue was the instability of dissolved oxygen (DO) which is controlled to maintain the bacterial reaction, DO effluent limits, and minimize energy costs. The owner alleged that they could not maintain a stable DO value for the reactor basins which was threatening their maximum effluent limits for DO and particulate.

Services Provided The solution to this element of the case involved process control of the DO aeration system. The plant was just operating too many aeration blowers. This resulted in the blowers operating outside of upper controllable range (i.e., too far up the performance curve) that with any slight increase in DO would cause a very high change in air flow. This caused a large drop in DO which of course caused a large drop in air flow. This was the observed cyclic nature of the problem.
Case Study 11  ·  Litigation Support

Steel Tube Manufacturing – Construction Claim

Situation

Construction disputes for a newly constructed tube mill resulted in the parties going to arbitration to settle their claims. The tube mill is a very complex construction project taking several years to complete. Construction claims included unresolved change orders, schedule delay claims and labor inefficiency claims due to labor stacking.

Services Provided Rimkus was retained to assist in the resolution of contested change orders and schedule delays related to mechanical field construction. This entailed the review of thousands of documents and records, preparation of cost estimates, assessing labor productivity, opine on industry standard practices for mill equipment alignment and critical path schedule delay analysis. At issue were revised alignment specifications for machined component tolerances, out of tolerance specification for owner supplied mechanical equipment, and piping fit-up issues for pre-fabricating piping supplied by the owner.
Case Study 12  ·  Investigation

OSHA - Personal Injuries

Situation

Workplace injuries are one of the leading types of lawsuits litigated in the US. When assessing responsible parties, litigants often turn to the OSHA regulations on workplace safe work practices to defend their position. This includes: Walking Working Surfaces (i.e., trips and falls), Lockout Tagout / Line Break / De-energization of Equipment, Hot Work, Confined Space, Fall Protection, and Personal Protection Equipment.

Services Provided Rimkus has experts in OSHA regulations and safe work practices and can support your case from site inspection, equipment failure root cause assessment, assessment of party's safe work practices and procedures, and worker training to expert reports and testimony.
